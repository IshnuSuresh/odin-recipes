Recipe: Sweet and Sour Chicken

Home

Description

Sweet and sour chicken is a popular Chinese-inspired dish featuring crispy, battered chicken pieces tossed in a vibrant, tangy-sweet sauce. The sauce is typically made with ingredients like vinegar, ketchup, soy sauce, and sugar, giving it its signature glossy red color and bold flavor. Often paired with chunks of pineapple, bell peppers, and onions, sweet and sour chicken balances savory crunch with sweet, zesty notes. Itâ€™s commonly served over steamed rice or noodles, making it a flavorful and satisfying main dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) boneless, skinless chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1 cup chopped bell peppers (red and green)
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup pineapple chunks (canned or fresh)
  • 1/3 cup ketchup
  • 1/3 cup rice vinegar or white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water (for thickening)

Steps

  1. Season the chicken pieces with salt and pepper.
  2. Coat the chicken in cornstarch, then dip each piece into the beaten eggs.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium-high heat and fry the chicken until golden and crispy. Remove and set aside.
  4. In a separate pan, add a small amount of oil and stir-fry the bell peppers and onions for 2-3 minutes until slightly softened.
  5. Add the pineapple chunks and cook for another minute.
  6. In a bowl, whisk together ketchup, vinegar, brown sugar, and soy sauce to make the sweet and sour sauce.
  7. Pour the sauce into the pan with the vegetables and bring it to a simmer.
  8. Stir in the cornstarch-water mixture and cook until the sauce thickens.
  9. Add the fried chicken back into the pan and toss to coat evenly with the sauce.
  10. Cook for 2-3 more minutes until the chicken is heated through and coated.
  11. Serve hot over steamed rice or noodles.

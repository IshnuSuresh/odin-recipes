Recipe: Lemon Icebox Pie

Home

Description

Lemon icebox pie is a cool, creamy dessert with a bright citrus flavor and a smooth, melt-in-your-mouth texture. Traditionally made with a graham cracker crust, the filling combines sweetened condensed milk, fresh lemon juice, and egg yolks or whipped cream to create a rich, tangy custard that sets in the refrigerator—no baking required. It's topped with whipped cream or meringue and chilled until firm, making it a perfect make-ahead dessert for warm days or any time you want something light, refreshing, and satisfying.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 cans (14 oz each) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice (about 3–4 lemons)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream (for topping)
  • 2 tablespoons powdered sugar (for whipped cream)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix graham cracker crumbs, granulated sugar, and melted butter until well combined.
  3. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom and sides of a 9-inch pie dish to form a crust.
  4. Bake the crust for 8–10 minutes, then let it cool completely.
  5. In a large bowl, whisk together sweetened condensed milk, lemon juice, lemon zest, and egg yolks until smooth and creamy.
  6. Pour the filling into the cooled crust and spread it evenly.
  7. Bake the pie for 15–18 minutes, just until the center is set.
  8. Remove from the oven and let the pie cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or until fully chilled.
  9. In a chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream with powdered sugar and vanilla extract (if using) until soft peaks form.
  10. Spread or pipe the whipped cream over the chilled pie before serving.
  11. Optional: Garnish with extra lemon zest or thin lemon slices.
